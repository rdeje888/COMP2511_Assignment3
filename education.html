My Education

I made some of my closest friends to this day while attending St. Isabella Junior High School for three years. The school provided an environment that fostered growth and development both academically and socially, and I enjoyed participating in extracurricular activities like sports teams and music programs. Even after all these years, I still keep in touch with many of my former classmates and cherish the memories we made together.

After St. Isabella, I attended Bishop O'Byrne High School for grades ten through eleven. However, in my senior year, I transferred to All Saints High School due to a move in houses. The transition was challenging, but I appreciated the smaller class sizes and personalized attention from teachers at All Saints. Both schools provided valuable opportunities for personal and academic growth, and I am grateful for the experiences I gained at each institution.

After finishing high school, I took a gap year to figure out what I wanted to do next. During that time, I discovered my passion for technology and eventually enrolled at Mount Royal University to study Computer Information Systems. The program challenged me to learn and apply new technical skills, and I enjoyed collaborating with my classmates. Looking back, I'm glad I took a gap year because it helped me find my direction and led me to a fulfilling career in tech.
